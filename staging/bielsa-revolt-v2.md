Marcelo Bielsa ended Uruguay's 22-year wait to beat Brazil in World Cup qualifying. Then his own players turned on him.
===
Bielsa took charge of Uruguay in 2023. He led them to the 2024 Copa America semifinals. He delivered World Cup qualification with historic wins over Brazil and Argentina at La Bombonera. The man was a hero.
===
The dressing room told a different story. Bielsa's training sessions were relentless. Double sessions in punishing heat. Film study past midnight. Players who complained were dropped. The Leeds cult hero had become a dictator in Montevideo.
===
Jose Maria Gimenez confronted Bielsa directly. The Atletico Madrid defender had seen enough. Senior players backed him. Younger squad members stayed silent. They were afraid of losing their World Cup spot.
===
Uruguay face Spain in a must-win Group H match. Saudi Arabia and Cape Verde are waiting. The squad is split down the middle. If Bielsa loses the dressing room completely, Uruguay's World Cup could be over before it starts.
===
Can a manager win a World Cup when his own players want him gone? Bielsa saved Uruguayan football. His obsession with control might destroy it.
